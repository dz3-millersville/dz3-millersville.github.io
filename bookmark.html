Bookmark (a.k.a Lithographic Printing)

Lithographic printing is the process of producing a large quantity of prints using the principals of the seperation of oil and water. Since water repels oil, the two can be used to allow oil based inks to adhere only to a designated area and then having that oil be transferred to a substrate.

In class, we utilized this printing process in order to mass produce bookmarks for a local elementary school. My design was grouped with others to maximize the number of bookmarks produced with each printed page. We then cut out each bookmark and sent them off to the school.

Reflecting on my Project

In review, I am very happy with how this project turned out. I wanted to accomplish creating a bookmark that played to the immagination of the students that would be utilizing these bookmarks. Thinking about the demographics that we were creating for, I believe I did a good job catering to elementary school students.
